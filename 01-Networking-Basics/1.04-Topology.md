# Network Topology

## Introduction

Network Topology refers to the physical or logical arrangement of devices within a network.

It defines:

- How devices are connected
- How data flows
- Network performance
- Fault tolerance

---

# Types of Topologies

1. Bus Topology
2. Star Topology
3. Ring Topology
4. Mesh Topology
5. Tree Topology
6. Hybrid Topology

---

# 1. Bus Topology

All devices are connected to a single cable called Backbone Cable.

Example:

PC1 ─ PC2 ─ PC3 ─ PC4

---

## Advantages

- Low cost
- Easy setup
- Requires less cable

---

## Disadvantages

- Single point of failure
- Difficult troubleshooting
- Performance decreases with more devices

---

# 2. Star Topology

All devices connect to a central switch or hub.

Example:

          Switch
        /    |    \
      PC1   PC2   PC3

---

## Advantages

- Easy management
- Easy troubleshooting
- Failure of one node doesn't affect others

---

## Disadvantages

- Switch failure affects entire network
- More cabling required

---

# 3. Ring Topology

Devices form a circular connection.

Example:

PC1 → PC2 → PC3 → PC4 → PC1

---

## Advantages

- Organized communication
- Predictable performance

---

## Disadvantages

- One failure can disrupt communication
- Difficult maintenance

---

# 4. Mesh Topology

Every device is connected to every other device.

---

## Example

A ↔ B

A ↔ C

A ↔ D

B ↔ C

B ↔ D

C ↔ D

---

## Advantages

- High reliability
- Multiple communication paths
- No single point of failure

---

## Disadvantages

- Expensive
- Complex setup

---

# 5. Tree Topology

Combination of:

- Star Topology
- Bus Topology

---

## Advantages

- Scalable
- Easy expansion

---

## Disadvantages

- Backbone failure impacts network

---

# 6. Hybrid Topology

Combination of multiple topologies.

Examples:

- Star + Bus
- Star + Ring

Used in modern enterprises.

---

# Comparison Table

| Topology | Cost | Reliability | Scalability |
|------------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Bus | Low | Low | Low |
| Star | Medium | High | High |
| Ring | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Mesh | High | Very High | Medium |
| Tree | Medium | High | High |
| Hybrid | High | High | Very High |

---

# Real World Usage

| Topology | Used In |
|------------|------------|
| Bus | Old Networks |
| Star | Homes & Offices |
| Ring | Telecom Networks |
| Mesh | Internet Backbone |
| Tree | Enterprises |
| Hybrid | Data Centers |

---

# Summary

Network topology determines how devices communicate. Star topology is the most commonly used today because of its simplicity, reliability, and scalability.
