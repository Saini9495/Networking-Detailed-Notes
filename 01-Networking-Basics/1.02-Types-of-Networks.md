# Types of Networks

## Introduction

Networks are categorized based on geographical coverage, size, and purpose.

The major types of networks are:

1. PAN
2. LAN
3. MAN
4. WAN

---

## 1. PAN (Personal Area Network)

PAN is the smallest type of network.

It connects devices around a single person.

### Coverage

Approximately 1–10 meters.

### Examples

- Bluetooth Headphones
- Smart Watch
- Mobile Hotspot
- Wireless Keyboard

### Advantages

- Easy Setup
- Low Cost
- Portable

### Disadvantages

- Limited Range
- Lower Speed

---

## 2. LAN (Local Area Network)

LAN connects devices within a limited area.

### Coverage

- Home
- School
- Office
- Building

### Examples

- Office Network
- Computer Lab
- Home WiFi

### Characteristics

- High Speed
- Low Latency
- Privately Managed

### Advantages

- Fast Communication
- Easy Resource Sharing
- Secure Environment

### Disadvantages

- Limited Coverage
- Maintenance Required

---

## 3. MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)

MAN covers a city or metropolitan region.

### Coverage

Several kilometers across a city.

### Examples

- City-wide Internet Service Provider Network
- University Campuses

### Characteristics

- Larger than LAN
- Smaller than WAN

### Advantages

- Connects Multiple LANs
- High Speed

### Disadvantages

- Expensive Setup
- Complex Management

---

## 4. WAN (Wide Area Network)

WAN covers large geographical areas.

### Coverage

- Countries
- Continents
- Entire Globe

### Example

The Internet

### Characteristics

- Uses Public and Private Networks
- Long Distance Communication

### Advantages

- Global Connectivity
- Resource Sharing Across Locations

### Disadvantages

- Higher Cost
- Higher Latency

---

## Comparison Table

| Feature | PAN | LAN | MAN | WAN |
|----------|------|------|------|------|
| Coverage | Few Meters | Building | City | Worldwide |
| Speed | Low | High | Medium-High | Variable |
| Cost | Low | Medium | High | Very High |
| Ownership | Personal | Private | Organization | Multiple Organizations |

---

## Enterprise Network Architecture

Real-world companies often use:

### LAN

Inside office buildings.

### MAN

Between branches within a city.

### WAN

Between offices across countries.

### PAN

- Bluetooth Earbuds

### LAN

### MAN

- ISP City Network

---

## Summary

Networks are classified based on size and coverage. PAN is the smallest, LAN connects local devices, MAN covers cities, and WAN provides global connectivity. Understanding these network types helps in designing scalable and efficient infrastructures.
